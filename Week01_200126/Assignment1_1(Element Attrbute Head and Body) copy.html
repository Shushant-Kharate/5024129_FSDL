Top 10 places to visit in India

1. Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Home to the Taj Mahal, the ultimate symbol of love. While the white marble mausoleum is the main draw, don’t miss the Agra Fort and the ghost city of Fatehpur Sikri nearby.

2. Jaipur, Rajasthan

Known as the "Pink City," Jaipur is a feast of royal history and vibrant markets. Highlights include the honeycomb-styled Hawa Mahal, the hilltop Amer Fort, and the City Palace. 2026 Highlight: Jaipur is currently reinventing itself as a modern design hub; look for DIY craft workshops in the new "Craft Districts."

3. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It is the spiritual heart of India, where you can witness the mesmerizing evening Ganga Aarti (fire prayer) on the banks of the River Ganges.

4. Kerala Backwaters (Alleppey & Kumarakom)

Often called "God's Own Country," this region is best experienced by hiring a traditional houseboat. You’ll float through tranquil, palm-fringed canals and lagoons while enjoying authentic Keralan cuisine prepared on board.

5. Leh-Ladakh, Ladakh

A high-altitude desert known for its rugged Himalayan landscapes, turquoise lakes like Pangong Tso, and ancient Buddhist monasteries. Trend Alert: Visit the Hanle Dark Sky Reserve (India’s first) for world-class stargazing and the Milky Way.

6. Goa

India's beach capital. North Goa is famous for its nightlife and water sports at Baga and Anjuna, while South Goa offers quiet, pristine sands like Palolem. Don't Miss: The UNESCO-listed Portuguese churches in Old Goa and the lush spice plantations inland.

7. Udaipur, Rajasthan

The "City of Lakes" is arguably India’s most romantic destination. The white-marble Lake Palace appears to float on Lake Pichola, and the sunset boat rides here are legendary.

8. Hampi, Karnataka

A UNESCO World Heritage site consisting of the ruins of the 14th-century Vijayanagara Empire. The landscape is surreal, filled with massive boulders and intricately carved temples like the Vittala Temple with its stone chariot.

9. Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

The "Yoga Capital of the World," situated in the foothills of the Himalayas. It’s a perfect blend of spirituality and adventure—you can attend meditation retreats or go white-water rafting on the Ganges.

10. Majuli, Assam

The world's largest river island, located in the Brahmaputra River. It is a breakout destination for 2026, perfect for "Slow Travel." It is famous for its unique Neo-Vaishnavite culture, traditional mask-making, and eco-friendly bamboo stays.
